What is aadhaar?

Aadhaar is the largest biometric identification scheme in the world. World Bank CEO Paul Romer described Aadhaar as “the world’s most sophisticated ID system.” Aadhaar has been the target of many decisions by India’s Supreme Court. The Supreme Court released an interim order on 23 September 2013 saying that “no-one will suffer for not getting Aadhaar“. Adding that, since it is voluntary and not mandatory, the government can not deny a service to a resident who does not own Aadhaar. It is a 12-digit unique identification number that residents or passport holders of India can voluntarily obtain based on their biometric and demographic data. The data is collected by the Unique Indian Identification Authority (UIDAI), a statutory authority founded by the Government of India in January 2009 under the jurisdiction of the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology, under the provisions of the Aadhaar Act (Targeted Delivery of Financial and Other Subsidies, Benefits and Services), 2016

How to Download aadhaar?

Having an e-Aadhaar card keeps your Aadhaar information handy and eliminates the possibility that you will ever lose or forget your Aadhaar number. It is appropriate across the country as it is a digital version of your standard Aadhaar, without exceptions. 

If you wish to download and print an e-Aadhaar card online, you must follow the steps below:

  1. Visit the official Aadhar website https:/uidai.gov.in/
  2. Click on the My Aadhaar choice ‘Download Aadhaar’ button or visit the https:/eaadhaar.uidai.gov.in / link
  3. Choose “Aadhaar” choice under section “I Have”
  4. Enter the Aadhaar number 12-digit now. If you want to download Masked Aadhaar click on ‘I want a masked Aadhaar’ link.
  5. Enter the Captcha Verification Code and press the “Return OTP” option to get your registered mobile number with the one time password
  6. Enter the OTP your mobile number has received.
  7. Complete the survey, and press “Verify and Install” to access your Aadhaar’s electronic file.

How to Check aadhaar Status?

Upon getting their slip of acknowledgment, one can check their aadhaar card status. The aadhaar card status can be reviewed either online or offline.To track the UIDAI aadhaar status, one must give the aadhaar enrollment number.

Individuals may check the status of an aadhaar card either by using the enrollment number or by using the enrollment number, by using the update request number or by using the acknowledgement / enrollment information, or by using the name or mobile number, or by using the online phone / mobile number verification.

  1. On the official UIDAI website, visit the following page: https:/resident.uidai.gov.in/check-aadhaar-status
  2. Provide your 14-digit number and date and time of registration.
  3. Enter the Safety Code
  4. Click on ‘Status Search’ button
  5. If you have created Aadhaar, you will be given an option to download it.
  6. Click on the ‘Aadhaar download’ option.

How to update aadhaar card?

Did you just change your username recently? Update your address online with relevant documents or a letter verifying your username. With the consent and authentication of the address verifier (a family member, parent, friends, and landlord) who is willing to let you use his address as evidence, you can update your Aadhaar address to where you currently reside.

As per UIDAI, an Aadhaar Cardholder’s demographic details are name, date of birth or age, address, gender, mobile number, and email identification. UIDAI has mentioned that biometric information is two image scans, a facial photograph, and ten Fingerprints. UIDAI charges a nominal price to its service updates. Although there are two cost-free services like Aadhaar Enrolment and compulsory Biometric updates. For Demographic updates, other than mandatory biometric updates, users need to pay Rs 50. Aadhaar search service using e-KYC / Find Aadhaar / any other A4 sheet tool and color printout would cost you Rs 30.

If you want a photo, fingerprint, iris scan, mobile, gender, e-mail updates, you have to pay Rs 50. According to UIDAI law, Aadhaar Cardholders wanting to update or change their Aadhaar address will need to pay Rs 50. To enroll for Aadhaar, you will be required to visit an Aadhaar Enrolment Center for yourself or your family member. If your Aadhaar demographic details (name, address, doB, gender, mobile number, email) are not up-to-date, you can update the same by visiting an Aadhaar Enrolment Center. Aadhaar holders kids (who turned 15) or others who need to update Biometrics details-Fingerprints, Iris & Photographs are also required to visit an Enrolment Center. Please get valid documents proving the Address. Check for the nearest Enrollment Center by selecting the following mode: State, Postal [PIN CODE], CheckBox.

Link aadhaar card with Pan card

Linking Aadhar with PAN has become compulsory because if your Aadhar is not linked to PAN your income tax return would not be processed. Also, whenever you need to perform a banking transaction above Rs 50,000, you need to have your PAN and Aadhar linked. It is really easy to link the PAN card with the Aadhaar card and the government has provided multiple ways to do that. There has been an aggressive campaign by the government over the last few months encouraging individuals to attach Aadhar card to PAN card online.  There are two things you need to consider as creditors and tax-assesses. Firstly, how to connect Aadhar Card online to PAN Card? Second, what are the advantages of connecting Aadhar to PAN.  As we know the PAN card is a unique tax identification number issued by the Department of Income Tax. This PAN is important not only for filing your IT returns but also for a number of other activities such as buying jewelry, buying houses, taking a loan, opening a bank account, open a Demat account, etc.

There are two ways you can connect PAN and Aadhaar cards. Such are:

  1. Via the e-filing website for income tax purposes.
  2. Send a text message to 567678 or 56161.

Link aadhaar card with mobile number

The government has made it mandatory to link with your mobile number as per the order passed by the Supreme Court. The main objective behind this step is to mitigate and filter out the connections that are released on fake documents. Re verification of mobile numbers with your Aadhaar has no charge and can be done in just a few seconds. 
  1. Go to the official portal of Aadhar here https://ask.uidai.gov.in/
  2. In the ‘login by’ option, select log in by  mobile number and enter your mobile number and captcha. Click on ‘Send OTP’ after you 
    have entered and checked the details.
  3. Next, Enter the received OTP on the right-hand side box and click ‘submit OTP and process. Always keep your mobile handy to receive and enter the OTP successfully.
  4. The next screen shows you the services offered by Aadhaar, viz. Current Aadhaar Enrolment and Review. Click on Aadhaar Update.
  5. The next screen will show you different fields such as, Name, Aadhaar Number, Resident Type, and what do you want to update, etc
  6. Since you want to link your mobile number to Aadhaar, fill in all the required fields and select ‘Mobile Number’ under the section ‘What do you want to update’. Click on proceed.
  7. Now you have to fill your mobile number and a captcha. Fill all the fields and click on ‘Send OTP’. Enter and verify the received OTP and click on ‘Save and Proceed’.
  8. Check all the details one last time and click on Submit.
  9. In the last, you will see a Success Screen with your Appointment id. Click on the ‘Book Appointment’ option to book a slot at visit the Aadhaar enrollment center.
